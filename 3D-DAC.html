3D Printed Engineered Structures for High Performance Direct Air Capture System


Contract Number : DE-FEE0032260

Project Description

The goal of the project is to make a manufacturing method for an already existing part that acts as a Direct Air capture device, but with additive manufacturing. The part will capture C02 through a rapid thermal swing adsorption (RTSA). It uses a dual head extruder, to swap materials layer by layer. You can find more info here.

CBP Specifics

TDA participates in the U.S. Department of Energy’s Phase II SBIR/STTR Diversity Supplement program that aims to increase the diversity in the SBIR programs’ participants. The purpose of this program is to improve the diversity of the research workforce by recruiting and supporting undergraduate and graduate students from groups that have been shown to be underrepresented in federal SBIR/STTR research programs. As part of this program TDA has hired a mechanical engineering intern for the summer of 2022 from an ethnic group that is underrepresented in the scientific workforce.

Our Partners

  • University of Miami

    UM has committed to increasing the participation of underrepresented groups in STEM, and will utilize Student Diversity, Outreach, and Women's Program at the University of Miami to recruit and promote such students for this project.

  • SLB

    SLB believes that one of its greatest strengths is the diversity of their workforce, with people of many nationalities and backgrounds working together and sharing common objectives. SLB does not have a 'nationality' that describes its culture but operates in a truly global manner throughout the world. SLB recruits and develops people in alignment with their business objectives and the countries in which they work.
